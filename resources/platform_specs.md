## Instagram (Primary Platform — ~3K followers)

**Video (Reels):**
- Aspect ratio: 9:16 (vertical — full screen)
- Resolution: 1080 x 1920px (iPhone native is fine)
- Length: 15–90 seconds (sweet spot for discovery: 15–30s; engagement: 30–60s)
- Audio: always add music or original audio — silent Reels underperform
- Cover frame: manually set a strong cover (do not leave as default first frame)

**Caption:**
- Max characters: 2,200 (but shorter performs better for Reels)
- Target length: 100–150 words
- First line is the only line visible before "more" — make it the hook
- Hashtags: place below main caption with a line break / dots separator
- Max hashtags: 30 (optimal: 20–25 mixed tiers)

**Carousel (static or mixed):**
- Up to 10 slides
- First slide = hook image (high contrast text, clean visual)
- Last slide = CTA (comment prompt, save prompt, DM prompt)
- Caption: up to 200 words, educational tone acceptable

**Best posting times (UK):**
- Tuesday–Thursday: 6–9pm
- Friday: 5–8pm
- Saturday: 9–11am
- Avoid: Monday all day, Sunday evening

**Algorithm notes:**
- Shares carry more weight than likes for Reel distribution
- Saves signal high-quality content to the algorithm
- Replays on Reels (looping) are tracked — a strong loop hook helps
- First 24 hours after posting are critical — respond to comments quickly

## TikTok (Secondary Platform — smaller following)

**Video:**
- Aspect ratio: 9:16 (vertical)
- Resolution: 1080 x 1920px
- Length: 15–60 seconds for reach; up to 3 minutes available but shorter performs better
- Audio: trending audio can significantly boost reach — check TikTok trending sounds weekly

**Caption:**
- Max characters: 2,200 (but keep to 150 characters for performance)
- Hook line must appear in caption — many users read caption before watching
- Hashtags: 3–5 only (not 30 like Instagram)
- Local hashtags do NOT help on TikTok — they limit discovery reach

**Text overlay:**
- First 2 seconds MUST have a text overlay hook (many viewers have sound off)
- Keep text large, high contrast (white on dark or dark on light)
- Position: bottom third of screen (avoids being cut by TikTok UI)

**Best posting times (UK):**
- TikTok algorithm is less time-sensitive than Instagram
- General guidance: 7am–10pm UK time — evenings slightly better
- Consistency matters more than exact time on TikTok

**Algorithm notes:**
- TikTok pushes to cold audiences much more aggressively than Instagram
- Completion rate is the key metric — if people watch to the end, it gets pushed further
- A good hook + strong retention = exponential distribution possible even for small accounts
- Comments (especially questions) signal engagement — respond to drive further reach

## Facebook (Community Platform)

**Video:**
- Same specs as Instagram (9:16, 1080 x 1920)
- Native video (uploaded directly, NOT YouTube link) performs significantly better
- Facebook Reels: same specs, similar format to Instagram

**Caption:**
- More storytelling is accepted and performs better than on Instagram
- Target length: 200–400 words for community/event posts; 100–150 for Reel captions
- Tone: more conversational, community-update style
- Tag location: always add "West Drayton" or "Uxbridge" as the post location

**Hashtags:**
- 3–5 max (Facebook hashtags have limited discovery value)
- Use Tier 1 (boxing-specific) + Tier 3 (local) only

**Posting strategy:**
- Cross-posting from Instagram is acceptable but rewrite the caption for Facebook's audience
- Boosted posts (paid): if boosting a Reel, keep caption shorter and CTA very clear
- Local groups: consider posting to relevant Facebook groups (Uxbridge community, West Drayton local groups, West London boxing groups) — use a community-appropriate tone (not promotional)

**Best posting times (UK):**
- Wednesday–Friday: 1–4pm (lunch / early afternoon)
- Saturday–Sunday: 10am–1pm (weekend browsing)

## YouTube Shorts (Lower Priority — use for strong tutorial or transformation content)

**Video:**
- Aspect ratio: 9:16 (vertical)
- Resolution: 1080 x 1920px
- Length: under 60 seconds for Shorts classification
- Longer videos (60s+) are regular YouTube uploads — different discovery mechanism

**Title:**
- Treat like a search query — include boxing + specific topic + outcome + location
- Example: "Boxing footwork for beginners — West London | Heathrow Boxing Club"
- Example: "What 30 days of boxing does to your body (West Drayton)"
- Max 100 characters

**Description:**
- 2–3 sentences
- Include: gym name, location (West Drayton, Uxbridge, West London), primary keyword phrase
- Add social links and first-class-free offer in description

**End screen / CTA:**
- End screens are NOT available for Shorts
- Use a pinned comment immediately after uploading: "First class free — DM us or come in: Heathrow Boxing Club, West Drayton"

**Algorithm notes:**
- YouTube Shorts gets different distribution from Instagram/TikTok — more search-driven
- Good titles = more discovery weeks and months after posting (evergreen potential)
- Tutorial and technique content tends to perform better on YouTube than on TikTok/Instagram

## Cross-Platform Posting Notes

**Do:** Upload natively to each platform (not sharing a link)
**Do:** Rewrite captions for each platform (do not copy-paste)
**Do:** Adjust hashtag count and selection per platform rules (see hashtag_bank.md)
**Don't:** Watermark content with one platform's logo before posting to another (TikTok watermark on Instagram hurts Reel reach significantly)
**Don't:** Use YouTube links on Facebook — native video outperforms embedded links
**Tool tip:** Use CapCut or Instagram's built-in editor to remove TikTok watermark if cross-posting
