Hook rules that apply to ALL types:

## Type 1: Curiosity Gap
*Withholds information to create the need to know more.*
*Best for: educational content, technique breakdowns, myth-busting, "did you know" moments.*

Templates:
```
"The [thing] most boxers get wrong about [topic]..."
"Nobody talks about what [training type] actually does to your [outcome]..."
"Why most people who start boxing never [negative outcome]..."
"The [technique/concept] that separates real boxers from fitness boxers..."
"We tried [unusual approach] for [timeframe] — here's what happened..."
"This is the part of boxing training that nobody films..."
```

Boxing-specific examples:
```
"The footwork mistake most beginners make in their first sparring session..."
"Nobody talks about what 3 months of pad work actually does to your reflexes..."
"Why most people quit the gym but stay in boxing..."
```

---

## Type 2: Transformation Claim
*Promises a before/after or measurable outcome. Shows results.*
*Best for: fitness seekers (Segment A), results content, month/week transformation series.*

Templates:
```
"[Timeframe] of boxing changed my [body part / skill / life]..."
"This is what [N] months of real boxing looks like..."
"Before boxing vs. after boxing — [specific outcome]..."
"[N] weeks in and [specific result]..."
"Started as a complete beginner. [Timeframe] later..."
```

Boxing-specific examples:
```
"8 weeks of boxing training — this is what happened to my body"
"Before boxing: couldn't last 2 minutes on the bag. After 6 weeks:"
"Started with zero boxing experience. 3 months later, first sparring session."
```

## Type 3: Direct Address
*Speaks directly to a specific person as if they were the only viewer.*
*Best for: local discovery, beginner conversion, targeting a specific audience segment.*

Templates:
```
"If you live in [area] and want to learn real boxing..."
"For anyone who thinks boxing is only for fighters..."
"If you've always wanted to try boxing but didn't know where to start..."
"This one's for anyone in [local area] looking for a real boxing gym..."
"If you're tired of boring gym workouts — this is for you..."
"For people in [area] who want to get fit without a treadmill..."
```

Boxing-specific examples:
```
"If you're in West Drayton or Uxbridge and want to actually learn boxing — watch this"
"For anyone in West London who's always wanted to try a real boxing gym..."
"This is for the person in Hayes or Hillingdon who keeps googling boxing classes..."
```

## Type 4: Controversy / Pattern Interrupt
*Challenges a common belief or disrupts expected content.*
*Best for: shares, comments, reaching new audiences, challenging generic gym culture.*

Templates:
```
"Boxing is NOT a fitness class. And that's why it works."
"Stop going to the gym. Do this instead."
"Why most gyms are setting you up to quit — and what boxing does differently..."
"The fitness industry doesn't want you to know this about boxing..."
"Regular gym: [negative]. Boxing gym: [positive contrast]."
"You've been working out wrong. Here's what boxing taught me."
```

Boxing-specific examples:
```
"Boxing is NOT a fitness class. Here's why that's the point."
"Stop lifting weights for fat loss. Try this instead."
"Why people who box consistently don't quit their fitness goals — but gym-goers do."
```

## Type 5: Question Hook
*Poses a question the viewer wants to see answered.*
*Best for: curious viewers, educational formats, driving watch time completion.*

Templates:
```
"What happens to your body after [N] weeks of boxing training?"
"Can a complete beginner walk into a boxing gym and keep up?"
"Is Ukrainian-style boxing actually different? We'll show you..."
"What does it feel like to spar for the first time?"
"How fast can you learn to box from zero?"
"Can boxing replace the gym for weight loss?"
```

Boxing-specific examples:
```
"What happens to your body after 30 days of boxing? We tested it."
"Can a complete beginner survive their first sparring session at Heathrow Boxing Club?"
"Is our Ukrainian-style coaching actually different from other boxing gyms?"
```

## Type 6: POV (Point of View)
*Places the viewer inside an experience. Immersive and aspirational.*
*Best for: lifestyle aspiration, beginner content, showing the gym atmosphere.*

Templates:
```
"POV: you just walked into your first boxing session"
"POV: first day at Heathrow Boxing Club"
"POV: you decided to try boxing instead of the gym"
"POV: it's been 6 weeks and you're already sparring"
"POV: West Drayton's best kept fitness secret"
```

Boxing-specific examples:
```
"POV: you walked into Heathrow Boxing Club for the first time"
"POV: you're 3 weeks in and just did your first pad round"
"POV: you swapped the gym for a real boxing club"
```

---

## Type 7: Number / List Hook
*Uses a specific number to imply organised, digestible value.*
*Best for: carousels, educational Reels, saves.*

Templates:
```
"[N] reasons boxing beats the gym for weight loss"
"[N] mistakes I made in my first month of boxing"
"[N] things nobody tells you before your first boxing class"
"[N] boxing drills you can do at home right now"
"[N] signs you're ready to start sparring"
```

Boxing-specific examples:
```
"3 reasons boxing beats the gym for fat loss — from someone who tried both"
"5 things nobody tells you before your first boxing class"
"The 3 technique fixes that improved my sparring immediately"
```
