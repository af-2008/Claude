## The Brand in Three Words
**Real. Technical. Community.**

Heathrow Boxing Club is not a fitness class with boxing gloves. It is a real boxing gym, in West Drayton, built around Ukrainian-style technique, proper coaching, and a community of people serious about the sport — from first-timers to fighters.

---

## Tone Descriptors

| Use This | Not This |
|----------|----------|
| Hype-driven and punchy | Overly corporate or formal |
| Technically credible | Bro-science or exaggerated claims |
| Community-proud | Exclusionary or intimidating |
| Direct and confident | Vague or hedge-worded |
| Aspirational but grounded | Fake motivational filler |
| Authentically boxing | Generic fitness influencer energy |

---

## Voice: Who Is Speaking

- **In hooks and CTAs:** Second person ("you") — speak directly to the viewer
- **For gym identity and community:** First person plural ("we", "our gym", "our coaches", "our members")
- **Never:** Third person about the gym ("Heathrow Boxing Club offers...") — sounds like a press release

## Phrases to Use

These phrases are on-brand and authentic to the gym:

```
"Real boxing"
"Proper technique"
"Ukrainian-style boxing"
"West Drayton's boxing gym"
"Come train with us"
"Earn it"
"The real thing"
"Our gym"
"Our coaches"
"Built for fighters, open to everyone"
"This is what real boxing looks like"
"No fitness class. Real boxing."
"From beginner to fighter"
"Train like a boxer"
```

---

## Phrases to Avoid

These undermine authenticity or sound generic/AI-generated:

```
"Transform your body in X days"
"Get shredded"
"No pain no gain" (overused cliché)
"We offer" (passive/corporate)
"World-class" (unverifiable, generic)
"State-of-the-art facilities"
"Join our family" (sounds like a franchise)
"Unlock your potential" (empty motivational filler)
"Level up" (overused)
"Your journey starts here" (generic gym cliché)
"We pride ourselves on" (corporate speak)
```

## Caption Length Targets

| Platform | Target Length | Notes |
|----------|--------------|-------|
| Instagram Reels | 100–150 words | First line = hook; hashtags below the caption |
| Instagram Carousel | Up to 200 words | Can include more detail or education |
| TikTok | Under 150 characters | Hook must appear in caption too |
| Facebook | 200–400 words | Storytelling tone, community angle |
| YouTube Shorts | 50–100 words description | Title is SEO priority |

## Emoji Guide

**Use these emojis — they fit the brand:**

| Emoji | Use for |
|-------|---------|
| 🔥 | Hype moments, intensity, training footage |
| 🥊 | Boxing-specific content (technique, sparring, pads) |
| 💪 | Fitness angle, transformation, results |
| 🎯 | Technique focus, precision, skill development |
| 🏆 | Results, fight wins, achievements, member success |
| ⚡ | Energy, speed, intensity |
| 👊 | Community, punching content |

**Emoji rules:**
- Max 3–4 emojis per caption
- Never open with an emoji (starts the caption weak)
- Never use emojis mid-sentence — keep them at the end of lines or as bullet separators
- Do not use: 😊 🙌 💯 (generic / overused by all brands)

## CTA Best Practices

**Always include a specific next step. Examples ranked by strength:**

Strong CTAs (for conversion):
- "DM us 'TRAIN' to book your first class"
- "First class free — message us or drop in: West Drayton"
- "Tag someone who needs to start boxing"

Medium CTAs (for engagement):
- "Save this if you're thinking about starting boxing"
- "Comment 'GYM' if you want the details"
- "What would you add? Drop it below"

Weak CTAs (avoid):
- "Follow us for more" (vague)
- "Check our bio" (unclear)
- "Like if you agree" (low value)

## Content Personality Examples

**On-brand post opening:**
"Most people who start boxing don't expect to still be training 6 months later. They come for the fitness. They stay for the sport."

**Off-brand post opening:**
"Looking to get fit and have fun? Our boxing classes are perfect for all levels!"

**On-brand CTA:**
"DM us 'TRAIN' — first class is on us."

**Off-brand CTA:**
"Visit our website to learn more about our classes and membership options."

## Platform Personality Adjustments

While the core voice stays consistent, the delivery shifts slightly per platform:

- **Instagram:** polished, hype-driven, community celebration, technique showcase
- **TikTok:** raw, authentic, slightly more entertainment-first, trend-aware
- **Facebook:** community news, event announcements, storytelling about members/coaches
- **YouTube Shorts:** educational-first, technique detail, "watch and learn" format
